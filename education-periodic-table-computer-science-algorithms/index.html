What the tile styles and badges mean

View — Landmarks: the ~38 ideas that orient you in the field. Core: the undergraduate-plus-systems baseline (~75). Full atlas: everything, including specialized tools and frontier research.

What earns a tile — an entry must teach a distinct, reusable computational idea that no existing tile represents better. Applications of other tiles, broad fields, cosmetic variants, products, and unsourceable claims are rejected. Tile size reflects canonicality — knowledge is not democratic.

Maturity — classic: textbook-stable · modern: in current production systems · research: important but mostly theoretical · speculative: emerging, may not last. Research/speculative tiles glow with dashed borders, like synthetic elements.

⚠ unreviewed — facts on this card have not yet been checked against the listed sources. Source-checked cards carry problem, mechanism, caveat, and references.

Growth — coarse navigation buckets, not formal bounds; open a card for the real best/average/worst rows.
